# User Interviews

Three conversations with potential users conducted during the assignment week.
Each interview was 10–15 minutes. Names/initials used with permission.

---

## Interview 1 — May 21, 2026

**Name:** [Use their initials or first name]
**Role:** Solo founder / indie builder
**Company stage:** Pre-revenue, no team

### Direct Quotes
- "I don't spend a single money on AI tools."
- "Since pro also gets out of responses... I was engaged with ChatGPT which helped me do another task."
- "First make money, then spend."
- "Unbiased and transparent... gives facts and figures then recommend."
- "Avoiding tools which are mainly tagged as sponsored."
- "A system which tracks your usage is considered genuine."

### Most Surprising Thing They Said
They don't pay for any AI tools at all — they actively juggle free tiers across
multiple tools as a coping mechanism for hitting usage limits. This was surprising
because I assumed the target user was already paying. It revealed a whole segment
(pre-revenue founders) who need a "free tier optimizer" path, not just a savings
calculator for paid plans.

### What It Changed About My Design
- Added an honest "you're spending well / optimally" state for $0 or near-zero
  spenders instead of forcing fake savings
- Decided the results page must show free tier alternatives alongside paid
  downgrades — not just paid-to-paid comparisons
- Trust signals (no sponsored recommendations, transparent reasoning) became
  a design priority on the results page, not an afterthought
- Credex CTA must not appear pushy for pre-revenue users — only surface it
  contextually for teams already spending significantly
